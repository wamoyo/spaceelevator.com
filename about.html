Meet Our Founder

Dr. Bradley Edwards, PhD

Physicist, Former Los Alamos National Laboratory

Lead Scientist, NASA Institute for Advanced Concepts (NIAC)

Dr. Bradley Edwards is the pioneering force behind modern space elevator development. As the lead scientist on NASA's Institute for Advanced Concepts feasibility study in 2003, he demonstrated conclusively that space elevator technology is not just possible—it's achievable with today's technology.

His groundbreaking NASA NIAC study resolved all major technological barriers and proved that current capabilities in carbon nanotube materials, electric climber vehicles, space debris avoidance systems, and fail-safe structural design make the space elevator a viable engineering project.

With a distinguished career at Los Alamos National Laboratory and decades of research in advanced materials and space systems, Dr. Edwards has dedicated his life to making space accessible to all humanity. He believes the space elevator will be the historic "breaking point" that historians will recognize 500 years from now as humanity's greatest technological achievement.

"There is no reason we can't build one today. We simply need the will to do it." - Dr. Bradley Edwards
