The Space Elevator - A revolutionary Earth-to-space transport system Get Book ↗

Dr. Bradley Edwards developed the first viable space elevator design and is the pioneering force behind modern space elevator development. Commissioned by NASA's Institute for Advanced Concepts, he tackled the fundamental engineering design challenges that had long made the space elevator seem impossible.

His comprehensive feasibility study in 2003 demonstrated conclusively that space elevator technology is not just possible—it's achievable with today's technology. The study resolved all major technological barriers and proved the space elevator is a viable engineering project using current capabilities in carbon nanotube materials, electric climber vehicles, powering beaming, space debris avoidance systems, and fail-safe structural design.

Currently, Dr. Edwards is developing mass production systems for long carbon nanotubes, including easy dispersion in liquids, resins and epoxies, and spinning them into continuous thread. These breakthroughs are now used for stronger plastics for 3D printing, conducting epoxies, stiffer composites, anodes, and advanced materials—the very technologies that make the space elevator possible.
