Today's Technology

Our breakthroughs in advanced carbon nanotube materials provide the final, missing piece

The space elevator represents humanity's most ambitious engineering project—a 100,000 km bridge extending from Earth's surface to beyond geosynchronous orbit, revolutionizing access to space.

Space Elevator Subsystems

Carbon Nanotube Ribbon

  • Ultra-high tensile strength materials
  • 100x stronger than steel at 1/6th the weight
  • Resistant to radiation and temperature extremes
  • Repairable from climbers

Electric Climber Vehicles

  • Laser-powered propulsion system
  • 7-day journey to geosynchronous orbit
  • Payload capacity: 20 tons per climber
  • Autonomous navigation and safety systems

Ocean-Based Station

  • Mobile platform for hazard avoidance
  • Evade space debris by repositioning Earth anchor
  • Lightning and storm avoidance
  • Ocean provides stability and isolation

Power Beaming System

  • Ground-based laser power transmission
  • Continuous energy for climber vehicles
  • Multiple laser beaming stations to avoid blockage
  • Less power needed as ascent proceeds

Geosynchronous Station & Counterweight

  • Main space station at geosynchronous orbit
  • Ribbon continues to constructed counterweight
  • Payload launching from counterweight
  • Giant slingshot to Luna, Mars, and beyond
